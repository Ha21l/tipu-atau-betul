# Tipu atau Betul? 🚩

> A browser game that trains you to spot real Malaysian scams before they fool you.

**[Live Demo](https://YOUR_USERNAME.github.io/tipu-atau-betul)** · Built for the Hack Club Stardance Challenge 2026

---

## What is this?

Tipu atau Betul? means "Scam or Real?" in Malay.

Each round, you are shown a suspicious message — a fake SMS from a bank, a WhatsApp prize notification, a Macau Scam phone call, a love scam opener. You decide if it is a scam or a legitimate message before the timer runs out.

Get it wrong and you lose money from your in-game RM 10,000 savings. Get it right and you protect a Malaysian victim from being scammed.

Scams cost Malaysians billions of ringgit every year. This game exists to make scam recognition feel like a skill, not just common sense.

---

## How to Play

1. Choose a difficulty — **Warga** (Easy), **Biasa** (Normal), or **Penganalisis** (Hard)
2. Choose a mode — **Classic** (10 cases) or **Survival** (infinite)
3. Read each message carefully
4. Click **SCAM** or **REAL** before the timer expires
5. Use the **Reveal Red Flag** hint button (3 per game) when stuck
6. Survive with as much of your RM 10,000 savings intact as possible

---

## Features

- Procedural scenario engine — thousands of unique message combinations, no two playthroughs are identical
- 12 scenario templates spanning 8 real Malaysian scam types
- Infinite Survival mode with no endpoint
- Countdown timer that mirrors the real pressure tactics scammers use
- Victim profile cards — each case has a named Malaysian citizen you are trying to protect
- Streak multiplier system — consecutive correct answers earn savings bonuses
- Scam category badges and end-game breakdown report
- Three difficulty levels with different timers and loss amounts
- Fully responsive — works on mobile

---

## Scam Types Covered

- Bank Phishing SMS
- Fake Prize and Lucky Draw scams
- Macau Scam (impersonating PDRM, MACC, Bank Negara)
- Love Scam
- Fake Job offers with registration fees
- Investment scams (Skim Cepat Kaya)
- Smishing (fake courier SMS)
- Impersonation scams (fake family member)
